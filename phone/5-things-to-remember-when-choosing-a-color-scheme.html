5 Things To Remember When Choosing A Color Scheme

Written by, Nicole Johnson

When it comes to weddings, or pretty much anything, color matters. It's what sets the mood. The icing on the cake, if you will. So, it's understandable why you'd feel the pressure of choosing the right ones for your wedding. Don't worry though. It's not as daunting a task as you might think. In fact, the following five tips might just help to make it a fun experience for you!

 

1) Think of What "Mood" You Want to Be In

 

When you envision your wedding, how do you want it to feel? Joyous? Nostalgic? Feminine? Perhaps a little funky? If you know how you want to feel at your wedding, then you'll be that much closer to choosing the right colors for it. Different colors sparks different emotions in us. They are utilized in advertising all the time to get us to buy stuff. You can do the same thing with your wedding. Find the specific emotion or feeling that you are going for and then you have a good start to picking the perfect color scheme.

 

2) Pay Attention to the Season

 

While you don't necessarily have to sprinkle leaves everywhere if your wedding is in the fall, using colors that tie in with the season in which your wedding will take place is definitely a good idea. So think about what season your wedding will be in and consider the specific colors associated with that season. Also, Google seasonal color schemes. You'll find lots of different combinations that can work for the same season. For example, just because your wedding may be in the winter, it does not mean you can't have a colorful wedding. With some creative thinking and a little research, you can find that set of colors that will work perfectly for you.

 

3) Don't Use Too Many Colors

 

When researching color schemes, especially on Pinterest, it's easy to want to incorporate ten different colors (or more) into your wedding. However, do not be tempted to do this. Just like too many cooks in the kitchen is a problem, too many colors in the same wedding color scheme is a problem. You don't want your wedding to look like a Ringling Brothers circus show. It's better to choose no more than four or five colors that will compliment each other and can be used on everything you need.

 

4) A Theme Always Helps

 

Hands down, the easiest way to figure out what colors you should use is by having a theme for your wedding. Your theme could be anything from a tropical theme that reminds you of a vacation you took in the past to perhaps a Harry Potter theme (just cuz you like the movie). Whatever theme you choose, make sure it comes from your heart and is something both you and your fiancé like. Once you've chosen your theme, then picking the colors becomes the easy part. Besides, the best weddings always have really good themes!

 

5) Make Sure the Colors Match Your Personality

 

Lastly and most importantly, make sure the colors speak to you as a person. That means knowing yourself and what kind of personality you have. This is the only way to create a wedding that you will look back on years from now and smile knowing that it was the greatest day you could've hoped for. After all, your wedding is about you and your fiancé. Nothing is a success unless the two of you are happy with it. Always keep that in mind and stay true to who you are. Do that and you won't have any problems picking out colors.

 

Remember these tips and you will be well on your way to discovering your perfect color scheme for your wedding! Also, the sooner you find it, the sooner you can share it with your vendors so they can incorporate those colors into everything you'll need for your wedding. Good luck!
