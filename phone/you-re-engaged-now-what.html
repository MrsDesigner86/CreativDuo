You're Engaged....Now What?

Written by, Nicole Johnson

Congratulations! You've found the love of your life and now you are engaged. A lifetime of love and compromise awaits you, but what do you do now? Where do you begin? While this article cannot give you all the answers to the questions you may have swirling around in your head, it can give you the basic steps to take so you are better prepared to take on this monstrous task of wedding planning. Even if you plan to hire a wedding planner, figuring some of these basics out beforehand will help make your wedding planner's job ten times easier.

 

Step #1: Pick A Date

 

Figuring out when to have your wedding is easier than you might think. Think of you and your fiancé. Are there any special dates that the two of you cherish, or even individually? What season would you rather get married in? You may think to yourself, "I have no idea and there's no reason to rush into picking a date now". Well actually, there is one reason to do it sooner than later even if you don't plan to marry for a couple years...and that reason is vendors.

 

Vendors typically require deposits wayy in advance of the wedding date and if you ever decide you want to use a very popular vendor who is always booked, you'll need to plan even further ahead. Even if you know it's going to be a while before the wedding takes place, choose a date anyway. You can always change it if need be and you'll never experience the disappointment of not being able to use the vendor you want because they were already booked prior to knowing about your wedding.

 

Step #2: Make A Preliminary Guest List

 

Take some time to sit down with your fiancé and discuss who you want to invite to your wedding. This doesn't have to be that extensive. Just think of the friends and family members that you really want there. Keep in mind to count any kids someone may have and their spouses. After you write down everyone who's important to you, take a step back and look at how many people are on the list. Are there too many names? Too few names? Tweak the list to a number that you both feel comfortable with because you will be paying for every single plate at your reception.

 

Once you're list is set and you are happy with the number of people, put it aside to take with you when you begin visiting venues and vendors...keeping in mind of course that the list may change before the big day. Doing this ahead of time will give you more information that you can later relay to a vendor because the most important thing they will need to know is how many guests will be there.

 

Step #3: Choose Your Theme & Colors

I know what you're thinking. This is the hardest task of them all, right? Not necessarily. It can seem that way but it doesn't have to be. This is actually the fun part! The part where you get to use your imagination and think about all the things that you love. What are your favorite hobbies? Favorite memories? Maybe even take a look at the decor in your home and see if any of those ideas may be a good fit for your wedding. The key is to create a wedding that speaks to your personality. Something that represents YOU. That's how you're going to get the wedding of your dreams.

 

If you struggle with choosing specific colors, be sure to read 5 Things To Remember When Choosing A Color Scheme to get you started.

 

Step #4: Begin Contacting Vendors

 

Now's the time to go vendor shopping! You can Google them or look through online vendor directories like TheKnot.com. Ask lots of questions about their services and narrow the choices down to a few that you love. Once you've picked your chosen few, it's time to start going on some field trips to visit the vendors in person (unless the vendor is a design business who can create your paper invitations and designs virtually like us). Seeing the vendors in person allows you to get a better feel for them as professionals and to see what their business looks like, especially if they are a venue! Gather as much information as you can so you can make an informed decision. The more you know upfront, the better.

 

Step #5: Choose Your Wedding Registry

 

Why do you need a registry? Because it's an easy way for people to get gifts for you and you also get discounts from the company you're registered with after the wedding. Win-win! How do you choose a registry? Easy. Just think of your favorite stores and decide which ones you want to create a registry account with. Keep in mind that not all of your guests will have the same sized pockets so it's best to choose a store that offers affordable items as well as higher-priced items. You can open your registry account online or in person at the store. They will also ask you for your wedding date.

 

After the registry is open, take some time to look through the store's website and add items that you would like others to get for you to the registry. This is not really mandatory but it does help give people ideas about what you want because you're telling them exactly what items you wish to have for your new life as a married couple.

 

Following these 5 steps will get you off to a great start in planning the wedding of your dreams with the least amount of hassle and stress. Congratulations on embarking on this new chapter in your life!
